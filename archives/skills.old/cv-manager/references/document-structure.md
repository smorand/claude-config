# Document Structure Template

## Overview

The tracking document contains multiple candidate entries, each following a strict hierarchical structure using Google Docs headings.

## Master Document Structure

**Document Title:**
- **Heading 1 (Title):** CVs

**Table of Contents:**
- **Heading 2:** Table of content

## Per-Candidate Structure Hierarchy

### Heading 2: Candidate Name
- Format: Full name of the candidate (e.g., "John Doe")
- One Heading 2 per candidate
- Each candidate is a separate Heading 2 section

### Heading 3: Presentation
This section contains the candidate's profile information.

**Required fields:**
- **CV Link:** URL to the CV file stored in Google Drive
- **Job Title:** Brief summary of the candidate's job title/role

**Description:**
- 100-200 words describing the candidate's background
- Include main skills, expertise, and experience highlights
- Focus on relevant qualifications for the position

### Heading 3: Interview
This section contains all interview records for the candidate.

### Heading 4: Interview Date
- Format: Date of the interview (e.g., "2025-01-15" or "January 15, 2025")
- One Heading 4 per interview session
- Interviews are listed chronologically

#### Heading 5: Attendees
- List of interview attendees as plain text (not bullets)
- Include names and roles if applicable
- One name per line

#### Heading 5: Summary
This section contains the interview summary with two distinct parts:

**Interview Content:**
1. **First paragraph: Overall Assessment**
   - Global feeling about the interview
   - Candidate's strengths observed during the interview
   - Candidate's weaknesses or areas of concern

2. **Second paragraph: Main Summary**
   - Key discussion points
   - Technical assessments
   - Notable responses or insights
   - Follow-up items or next steps

## Example Structure

```
# CVs (Heading 1 - Document Title)

## Table of content (Heading 2)

## John Doe (Heading 2)

### Presentation (Heading 3)

**CV Link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/xxx/view
**Job Title:** Senior Software Engineer

John Doe is an experienced software engineer with 8+ years in full-stack development. His expertise spans Python, JavaScript, and cloud architectures (AWS, GCP). He has led multiple teams in building scalable microservices and has strong experience in DevOps practices. Notable achievements include reducing deployment time by 60% and implementing CI/CD pipelines for enterprise applications.

### Interview (Heading 3)

#### January 15, 2025 (Heading 4)

##### Attendees (Heading 5)
Sebastien Morand (CTO)
Jane Smith (Engineering Manager)
Mike Johnson (Senior Developer)

##### Summary (Heading 5)
Overall, the interview was positive with John demonstrating strong technical knowledge and communication skills. His main strength is his deep understanding of distributed systems and cloud architecture. A potential weakness noted was limited experience with our specific tech stack (FastAPI), though he showed willingness to learn.

The discussion covered John's experience with microservices architecture, particularly his work on a high-traffic e-commerce platform. He explained his approach to system design, emphasizing scalability and maintainability. Technical deep-dive into his recent project revealed solid understanding of database optimization and caching strategies. Next steps: technical assessment and second round with the team.
```

## Key Notes

- **Document Title:** "CVs" is Heading 1 (only one in the entire document)
- **Table of content:** Heading 2 (inserted at document start)
- **Candidates:** Each candidate is a Heading 2 section
- **Attendees Format:** Plain text list, not bullets
- **Summary Format:** Two paragraphs - overall assessment first, then detailed discussion
- **Insertion Point:** New candidates inserted after "Table of content" section
